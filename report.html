Title:

Investigating the Reasons for Student Cheating and Exploring the Effectiveness of Safe Exam Browser in Minimizing Cheating during Examinations

Abstract:

The issue of student cheating during examinations has become a major concern in the academic world, and this has called for the need to explore possible measures to curb the menace. One such measure is the use of safe exam browser, which has been found to be an effective tool in minimizing cheating during examinations. This research proposal seeks to investigate the effectiveness of safe exam browser in curbing student cheating during examinations.

Introduction:

Student cheating during examinations has been a long-standing issue in the academic world, with several studies pointing to the prevalence of the problem. This problem has been attributed to various factors, such as the pressure to perform well, lack of preparation, and the availability of technology. One effective solution that has been suggested is the use of safe exam browser, which is a software that limits access to the internet and other applications during an examination.
Cheating can take many forms, including plagiarism, copying from other students, using unauthorized materials, and using electronic devices. Cheating undermines the integrity of education and devalues academic achievements. Many institutions have implemented measures to prevent cheating, but there is still a need to explore new methods to minimize cheating during examinations. One promising approach is the use of Safe Exam Browser, which restricts access to the internet and other computer applications during examinations. This research report sought to investigate the effectiveness of safe exam browser in minimizing student cheating during examinations.

Objectives:

The objectives of this research were to:

  1. Investigate the prevalence of student cheating during examinations
  2. Determine the effectiveness of safe exam browser in minimizing student cheating during examinations
  3. Identify the factors that influence student cheating during examinations
  4. Examine the perceptions of students and lecturers towards the use of safe exam browser
  5. Make recommendations on the effective use of safe exam browser in minimizing student cheating during examinations.

Hypothesis:

H1: There is a significant relationship between the use of SEB and reducing cheating during exams.
H2: Students' perceptions of SEB positively influence the effectiveness of the application in reducing cheating during exams.
H3: The use of SEB during examinations will significantly reduce the incidence of academic cheating among students.

Methodology:

This research project employed a mixed-method approach, combining both quantitative and qualitative methods. A survey was administered to students to collect quantitative data on cheating behavior and student engagement. In addition, qualitative data was collected through focus group discussions with students and faculty members. The research was conducted at a university of Lays Advantists of Kigali (UNILAK), Kigali campus. The study was conducted during the fall semester of 2023, and the sample size was determined using a random sampling technique.
This research adopted a mixed-method approach, which involved both quantitative and qualitative data collection and analysis. The research involved a survey of students and lecturers to collect data on the prevalence of student cheating, the effectiveness of safe exam browser, and the factors that influence student cheating.
The study utilized also a mixed-method approach, incorporating a questionnaire survey and semi-structured interviews. The questionnaire survey was administered to a sample of students, randomly selected from the university's population. The semi-structured interviews was conducted with students, lecturers, and invigilators to explore their experiences with cheating during exams and the effectiveness of SEB. Additionally, interviews and focus group discussions were conducted to explore the perceptions of students and lecturers towards the use of safe exam browser. Data was analyzed using descriptive and inferential statistics, and the findings were presented in tables, graphs, and charts, content analysis, and thematic analysis.
