About:

Safe exam browser is a program that provides a secure environment for taking online exams. SEB is a web browser specifically designed to create a secure environment for taking online exams. It is intended to prevent cheating and protect the validity of the exam process. During the exam, SEB locks down the system, stopping the student from accessing other applications or websites. SEB also has features that enable teachers to observe student behavior and identify any attempts to cheat. This can help guarantee that exams are conducted fairly and that students take responsibility for their actions. SEB runs on a local computer and it is connected via the internet to a learning management system (LMS) or an e-assessment system. Generally, SEB works with any web-based LMS and other kinds of web-based exam systems. Some learning management systems (LMS) for example Model, ILIAS, OpenOlat, and exam solutions such as Inspera Assessment offer a quiz mode specifically compatible with SEB. SEB does not connect to any central server or cloud service! SEB Server is being developed as an independent open-source software component that can be installed on a self-operated server. SEB clients can then be connected to such a SEB server instance using individual configuration files. This is strictly optional and must be configured individually in any case. This also applies to the support and proctoring capabilities in new SEB versions via video conferencing services such as Jitsi Meet (open source, can be installed on a self-operated server) or Zoom (commercial, cloud-based). This video conferencing connection must also be configured individually and is always disabled by default. If a SEB client has been configured to connect to a video conferencing service or a SEB server instance, this will be indicated to candidates when starting an exam session and video/audio proctoring/support must be explicitly agreed to by each exam candidate in a dialog box.

Benefit of SEB:

SEB can help to reduce cheating in the classroom by providing a secure environment for taking exams. It also allows teachers to monitor student activity and detect any attempts at cheating. This can help to ensure that exams are fair and that students are held accountable for their actions. SEB also helps to promote academic integrity and encourages students to take their studies seriously. It can also help to create an environment of trust and respect between teachers and students, which can lead to better learning outcomes.

Limitation of SEB:

While SEB can help to reduce cheating in the classroom, it has limitations. It can be difficult to configure and may require additional hardware and software to be installed. It also requires a certain level of technical expertise to set up and use. SEB can also be bypassed by tech-savvy students, and it may not be able to detect all attempts at cheating. As such, it is important to have other measures in place to ensure the integrity of the exam process.

Appendix:

Appendix A: Pressure to Perform: One of the main reasons why students cheat is the pressure to perform. There is a lot of emphasis on grades, and students feel that they need to do well in order to succeed in their academic and professional careers.
i. Lack of Understanding: Sometimes, students cheat because they do not understand the material. They feel that cheating is the only way to pass the exam or complete the assignment.
ii. Laziness: Some students cheat simply because they are lazy. They do not want to put in the effort to study or complete assignments, so they take shortcuts.
iii. Lack of Consequences: If students do not face any consequences for cheating, they are more likely to continue cheating. This can happen if teachers do not take cheating seriously or if the penalties for cheating are not severe enough.
iv. Peer Pressure: Students may also cheat because of peer pressure. They may feel that they need to cheat in order to fit in with their friends or because their friends are also cheating.
